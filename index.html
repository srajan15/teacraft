Health Benefits of Tea

Antioxidant Power

Indian teas, especially green and black teas, are packed with antioxidants that help combat free radicals, promoting cellular health and slowing down the aging process.

Boost Immunity

Ingredients like ginger and turmeric in masala chai and herbal teas strengthen the immune system, helping the body resist common colds and illnesses.

Aids Digestion

Herbal teas like peppermint and ginger, commonly blended in Indian teas, have natural digestive properties, easing stomach discomfort and promoting better digestion.

Promotes Relaxation & Reduces Stress

Teas like chamomile and tulsi are known for their calming effects. A warm cup can help reduce anxiety and encourage restful sleep.

Types of Indian Teas & Their Unique Benefits

Masala Chai

A traditional Indian blend of black tea with spices like cardamom, ginger, and cinnamon. Known to improve circulation, aid digestion, and provide an energizing boost.

Darjeeling Tea

Often called the “Champagne of Teas,” Darjeeling tea has a light, floral aroma and is rich in antioxidants. It’s great for heart health and has gentle, uplifting effects.

Assam Tea

Bold and malty, Assam tea has high caffeine content, making it an ideal morning tea. It’s also a natural source of polyphenols, which help in reducing inflammation.

Tulsi Tea (Holy Basil)

Known for its adaptogenic properties, tulsi tea helps the body manage stress and promotes overall wellness. It’s caffeine-free and packed with nutrients.
